Astrophysics

Our understanding of the universe has grown exponentially over the last few centuries. In the ancient times, we never believed in science and instead believed in myths, the most famous ones from Greece. Then, philosophy emerged and the first scientists began asking scientific questions. Plotemy came up with the idea that Earth is at the centre of the universe. At first, this seemed like the logical answer, however, as more and more accurate measurements contradicted the model, the planetary model took over. Copernicus, Galileo and Kepler showed that the Earth is not at te centre, but instead the Sun is. Isaac Newton revolutionised how we think about gravity. However, small errors emerged in the theory of gravity and so Einstein's Theories of Relativity restored those errors back to 0. It also predicted the beginning of the universe, which we call the Big Bang. Quantum Mechanics explains the behaviour of particles on the atomic level. Now we are at a stage where we need to create a theory of everything which unifies both Quantum Mechanics and General Relativity. This is a very hard thing to do but there has been progress.

The Environment

The Earth was formed from a protoplanetary disk 4.5 billion years ago. It was a toxic world, but all of that was going to change. Around 4.4 billion years, a frozen world, around the size of Mars, collided with the Earth causing it to obliterate into pieces. But then a new planet emerged, our Earth, and as it cooled, it had the conditions to start life. Now the Earth is teeming with life and it has transformed the face the Earth. However, around 2 million years ago, a new, intelligent species emerged. This species would later build architecture and start the chain reaction to extinction. And it's still happening to this day. That species is the homo sapiens.
Mankind is currently causing an extinction event that will eventually kill all species on Earth, including a large amount of us, homosapiens . This is because of us hunting down and killing animals just because of their resources. Moreover, marine fishing and deforestation are destroying not only the animals, but the environment around it.
We need to do something about our planet, because so far this is our only home. We need to take extra care of our planet and we want to make our planet the best planet.

How am I changing the World

I and many other students with similar ambitions are working alongside our local council to change the future and make our urban environment more sustainable. We are conducting several sustainability projects across our neighbourhood, encouraging kids and adults to join us along. These projects include -

  • Using GPS trackers to monitor and save kangaroos
  • Encouraging kids to walk to school by following a robot
  • Monitoring the water quality in the Plenty River to save various wildlife species
  • Monitoring how efficiently we are using rainwater
